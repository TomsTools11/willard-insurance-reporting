Bid strategy

Priced to win the value positions

Base bid
$6.65
Set to the live market cost of the highest-volume mid-priced channel in Missouri auto.
Effective bid range
$4.30–$7.30
What we actually bid per channel after tuning each one to its position.
Blended cost / click
~$5.40
Weighted across the channels carrying the volume.
Positioning approach
We deliberately price to the third position on the ad wall rather than fighting for the top spot. Non-standard shoppers actively scroll and compare, so a value position still captures the click, at a meaningfully lower cost than the carriers paying up for first.
Channel concentration
Budget is concentrated on the high-volume, value-priced channels: referral networks, email, the paid-search variants, and native and social. This is where the non-standard inventory the larger carriers overlook actually lives.
What we hold back
The premium search channels run $30 to $55 per click. The economics do not fit a non-standard book, so we keep those throttled down rather than letting them drain the daily budget.
Why a $6.65 base when leads price lower? The base bid is the anchor we tune from, not the price you pay on most clicks. After we step the priority channels down to their value positions, the realized cost lands near $5.40 per click, with the cheapest productive channels closer to $4.30.

06

Measurement plan

The right metric at the right time

Days 0–14
Contact Rate & Pacing
Are you reaching the leads quickly, and is the $50 daily budget pacing cleanly across the day? Speed to lead is the earliest signal of a healthy campaign.
Days 15–30
Quote Rate
Of the leads you reach, how many become quote-worthy? Channel patterns emerge here, and we begin tuning bids and timing against them.
Days 30–60
First CPA Read
The first reliable cost per bound policy, once leads have had time to move through quoting and binding. We calibrate bids against your real close data.
Days 60–90
Return & Scale
Sustained CPA and premium trend. We decide what to scale, what to refine, and whether to widen hours or budget.
Because this campaign is brand new, we judge the first few weeks on contact rate and quote rate, not cost per acquisition. Bound policies take time to mature, so an early CPA number would be misleading. We will walk through these together at your biweekly reviews, Tuesdays at 2:00 PM CT.
One standing action from you: export your full quote & bind data from AgencyZoom and send it to Tom at least 24 hours before every review. For your first review, the export should cover everything from when we started through the current date. Going forward, each export only needs to span the window between your previous review and the current one. This data is what lets us measure true performance — quote rate, bind rate, and cost per bound policy — and tune the campaign around the leads that actually convert for you, so getting it over a day ahead keeps every review focused on decisions rather than data gathering.
