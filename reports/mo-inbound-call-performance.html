Inbound call metrics, May 24 – July 6, 2026.

Between May 24 and July 6, 2026, prospects placed 32 inbound calls to the agency. 22 were answered (68.75%). Answered calls produced 21 quotes and 2 bound policies across 9.1 hours of talk time. Of the 10 unanswered calls, 8 rang 32 seconds or longer before disconnecting.

An inbound call is 2.8× more likely to reach a live quote than the typical MO Commercial touch (65.6% vs. 23.3%) — prospects who call in are further along and easier to engage. Once quoted, campaign leads are currently closing at a higher rate (37.5% vs. 9.5%), though the call sample is small (21 quotes vs. 40); see recommendation 4. Inbound calls and the MO Commercial campaign are separate, overlapping-but-not-identical populations and date ranges — this comparison is directional, not a controlled test.
05

Recommendations

1
Answer inbound calls within 30 seconds
Every call answered inside 30 seconds was picked up. 8 of the 10 missed calls rang 32 seconds or longer before disconnecting.
2
Add backup answering for overflow
Route calls that ring past roughly 30 seconds to a second line or team member so they are not lost at the 32-second mark.
3
Return every missed call with a callback and text
10 calls went unanswered in the period. A prompt callback and text after a missed call raises the chance of reconnecting.
4
Hold the current quoting motion on answered calls
21 of 22 answered calls reached a quote (95.5%). Answer rate is the primary lever between call volume and quotes.
5
Review the call-to-bind handoff
Calls convert to a quote far more often than the average campaign touch, but bind rate on those quotes trails the campaign average (9.5% vs. 37.5%). With only 21 quoted calls to date, watch this over the next period before changing the follow-up process.
